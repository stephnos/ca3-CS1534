History

Our Story

Background information about San Francisco's Past

The Gold Rush


In 1848, gold was found northeast of San Francisco and started the California Gold Rush. This turned what was once a small settlement of about 200 residents in 1846, to a booming city of 36,000 by 1852.

Alcatraz Island

Alcatraz is considered a "must do" to most tourists visiting SF. Home to many famous gangsters including Al Capone, the island was declared a national landmark in 1986.

The Great Earthquake and Fire of 1906

San Francisco was hit by a huge earthquake in April of 1906 (7.9 on the Richter Scale). The result was about 80% of San Francisco being completely destroyed.

The Summer of Love

A phenomenon where hundreds of thousands of people dressed like hippies and converged in our Haight-Ashbury neighborhood.
